# Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** NCS-POL-002  
**Version:** 2.2  
**Effective Date:** 01 September 2024  
**Policy Owner:** CISO  
**Approved By:** CEO  
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines acceptable and unacceptable use of NexaCloud Solutions' information systems, equipment, and network resources.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors with access to NexaCloud systems.

---

## 3. Acceptable Use

Employees may use NexaCloud systems for:
- Work-related tasks and business activities
- Incidental personal use that does not consume significant resources, affect productivity, or violate any policy statement below

---

## 4. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on NexaCloud systems and networks:

### 4.1 Security Violations
- Attempting to access systems, accounts, or data without authorization
- Circumventing or disabling security controls (firewall, antivirus, MDM, MFA)
- Installing unauthorized software or tools, including dual-use security tools, without prior approval from IT Operations
- Storing encryption keys, API keys, or passwords in plain text in files, code, or notes

### 4.2 Data Handling Violations
- Storing company or customer data on personal devices, personal cloud storage, or unauthorized external services
- Sending Restricted or Confidential data via personal email or unsecured channels
- Sharing customer data with unauthorized third parties
- Removing or disabling data classification labels from documents

### 4.3 Illegal or Unethical Activities
- Downloading or distributing pirated software, media, or copyrighted materials
- Accessing or distributing illegal content of any kind
- Using company systems to conduct personal business activities
- Harassment, discrimination, or hostile communications via company systems

### 4.4 Network and Infrastructure Misuse
- Using company network or systems to mine cryptocurrency
- Using company resources to attack or disrupt other systems (internal or external)
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network
- Disabling endpoint security software (CrowdStrike, MDM)

---

## 5. Monitoring

NexaCloud reserves the right to monitor activity on its systems, networks, and devices to the extent permitted by applicable law. Employees should have no expectation of privacy on company-owned devices or systems.

---

## 6. Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination and legal action where applicable.
